Coffee Recipe

Follow these steps for a clean, tasty brew. The main steps are numbered, and the tips are bullet points.

  1. Start with good beans
    • Choose fresh, high-quality whole beans (not pre-ground).
    • Look for a roast date within the last 2–4 weeks.
    • Store beans in an airtight container, away from heat and light.
  2. Use clean, fresh water
    • Use filtered water if your tap water tastes like chlorine.
    • Use water at 90–96°C (195–205°F) — just off the boil.
  3. Grind right before brewing
    • Coarse → French press
    • Medium → Drip / pour-over
    • Fine → Espresso
    • Grinding fresh keeps aroma and flavor stronger.
  4. Measure your ratio
    • Start with 1 part coffee : 15–18 parts water (Example: 20 g coffee → 300 ml water).
  5. Brew with control
    • Pour-over: pour slowly in circles.
    • French press: steep 4 minutes, then press.
    • Espresso: extract in 25–30 seconds.
    • Keep equipment clean for best taste.
  6. Taste and adjust
    • Too bitter → use a coarser grind or less brew time.
    • Too sour → use a finer grind or longer brew time.
    • Adjust the coffee-to-water ratio to preference.
  7. Serve immediately
    • Coffee tastes best right after brewing.
    • Pre-warm your cup to keep it hot longer.
