Coffee Recipe

  1. Start with good beans
    • Choose fresh, high-quality whole beans (not pre-ground).
    • Look for a roast date within the last 2–4 weeks.
    • Store beans in an airtight container, away from heat and light.
  2. Use clean, fresh water
    • Use filtered or bottled water if your tap water is of poor quality.Filtered water tastes better than tap water with chlorine.
    • Ensure the water is at the right temperature (195–205°F or 90–96°C) for optimal extraction.
    • Ideal temperature: 90–96 °C (195–205 °F) — just off the boil.
  3. Grind right before brewing
    • Match grind size to your method:
    • Coarse → French press
    • Medium → Drip / pour-over
    • Fine → Espresso
    • Fresh grinding keeps aroma and flavor strong.
  4. Measure your ratio
    • Standard starting point: 1 part coffee : 15–18 parts water (Example: 20 g coffee → 300 ml water)
  5. Brew with control
    • Pour-over: pour slowly in circles.
    • French press: steep 4 minutes, then press.
    • Espresso: extract in 25–30 seconds.
    • Keep equipment clean for best taste.
  6. Taste and adjust
    • Too bitter → use coarser grind or less brew time.
    • Too sour → use finer grind or longer brew time.
    • Adjust coffee-to-water ratio to preference.
  7. Serve immediately
    • Coffee tastes best right after brewing.
    • Pre-warm your cup to keep it hot longer.
